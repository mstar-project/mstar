The main causes of World War I were militarism, alliances, imperialism, and nationalism. Militarism was the belief that a nation should have a strong military and be prepared to use it aggressively. Alliances were agreements between countries to support each other in times of war. Imperialism was the practice of one country taking control of another country or region. Nationalism was the belief that one's own country is superior to all others. These four factors combined to create an environment in which war was seen as a viable option for resolving disputes.
