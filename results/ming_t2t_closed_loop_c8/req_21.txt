Occam's Razor is a principle that suggests that when presented with competing hypotheses or explanations, the one with the fewest assumptions should be selected. In other words, the simplest explanation is usually the best one. This principle is named after William of Ockham, a 14th-century English philosopher and theologian who popularized it. The idea is that if two theories explain the same phenomenon equally well, the simpler one is preferable because it makes fewer assumptions and is therefore less likely to be wrong. However, it's important to note that Occam's Razor is not a rule of logic, but rather a heuristic or rule of thumb that can be useful in guiding scientific inquiry and decision-making.
