The human immune system is a complex network of cells, tissues, and organs that work together to defend the body against pathogens such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites. It also helps to identify and destroy abnormal cells, such as cancer cells. Here's a simplified overview of how the immune system works:

1. **Recognition**: The immune system can recognize foreign substances (antigens) that are not normally present in the body. These antigens can be parts of pathogens or other foreign materials.

2. **Innate Immunity**: This is the first line of defense and includes physical barriers like the skin and mucous membranes, as well as non-specific immune responses. Cells like macrophages and neutrophils engulf and destroy pathogens. Natural killer (NK) cells can also kill infected cells.

3. **Adaptive Immunity**: This is a more specific and targeted response that develops over time. It involves two main types of cells: B cells and T cells.
