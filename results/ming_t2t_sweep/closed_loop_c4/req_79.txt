The key principles of object-oriented programming (OOP) are encapsulation, abstraction, inheritance, and polymorphism. Encapsulation is the practice of bundling data and methods that operate on that data into a single unit, called an object. Abstraction is the process of hiding the implementation details of an object from the user. Inheritance is the ability of one class to inherit the properties and methods of another class. Polymorphism is the ability of objects to take on multiple forms.
