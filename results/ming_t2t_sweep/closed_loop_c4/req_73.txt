Dark matter is a type of matter that does not interact with light or other forms of electromagnetic radiation, making it invisible to telescopes and other instruments that detect light. It is thought to make up about 85% of the matter in the universe, but its exact nature is still unknown. Scientists believe that dark matter exists because of its gravitational effects on visible matter, such as stars and galaxies. The presence of dark matter is inferred from its gravitational influence on the motion of galaxies and the large-scale structure of the universe. Despite extensive research, the true nature of dark matter remains one of the biggest mysteries in modern physics.
