Wi-Fi is a wireless networking technology that allows devices to connect to the internet without the need for physical cables. It works by using radio waves to transmit data between a device and a wireless access point, which is typically connected to a wired network and the internet.

Here's a simplified explanation of how Wi-Fi works:

1. **Wireless Access Point (WAP):** A WAP, also known as a router, is a device that connects to a wired network and broadcasts a wireless signal. It acts as a bridge between the wired network and the wireless devices.

2. **Radio Waves:** The WAP uses radio waves to transmit data. These waves are part of the electromagnetic spectrum and can travel through the air. Wi-Fi uses specific frequencies within this spectrum, typically 2.4 GHz or 5 GHz, to avoid interference from other devices.

3. **Data Transmission:** When a device wants to send data over Wi-Fi, it sends a request to the WAP. The WAP then transmits the data over the air using radio waves.
