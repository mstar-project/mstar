A hypothesis is an educated guess or an idea that can be tested through experimentation or observation. It is a tentative explanation for a phenomenon or a set of observations that can be proven or disproven through further investigation. A hypothesis is often used as a starting point for scientific inquiry and is typically based on existing knowledge or data.

On the other hand, a theory is a well-substantiated explanation of some aspect of the natural world that is acquired through the scientific method and repeatedly tested and confirmed through observation and experimentation. A theory is a comprehensive framework that explains a wide range of phenomena and is supported by a large body of evidence. It is not just a guess or a hunch, but a well-established explanation that has been rigorously tested and refined over time.

In summary, a hypothesis is a proposed explanation that can be tested, while a theory is a well-supported explanation that has been extensively tested and is widely accepted in the scientific community.
