Noise-canceling headphones use a combination of passive and active noise cancellation to reduce unwanted ambient sounds. Passive noise cancellation is achieved through the physical design of the headphones, which blocks out external noise by creating a seal around the ears. Active noise cancellation uses microphones to pick up external noise and then generates an opposite sound wave to cancel it out. This process is known as destructive interference, where two sound waves with opposite phases combine to create a quieter environment.
