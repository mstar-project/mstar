A hypothesis is an educated guess or a proposed explanation for a phenomenon, while a theory is a well-substantiated explanation of some aspect of the natural world that is based on a body of facts that have been repeatedly confirmed through observation and experimentation. In other words, a hypothesis is a tentative explanation that can be tested, while a theory is a comprehensive explanation that has been extensively tested and supported by evidence. A hypothesis can become a theory if it is supported by enough evidence and is widely accepted by the scientific community.
