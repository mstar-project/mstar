The Fermi Paradox is a question that asks why, given the vast number of stars in the universe and the high probability of planets capable of supporting life, we have not yet found any evidence of extraterrestrial civilizations. The paradox is named after physicist Enrico Fermi, who famously asked, "Where is everybody?" during a discussion about the possibility of extraterrestrial life. The paradox highlights the apparent contradiction between the high probability of the existence of extraterrestrial civilizations and the lack of evidence for their existence.
