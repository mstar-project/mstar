The mitochondria is known as the powerhouse of the cell. It is responsible for producing energy in the form of ATP (adenosine triphosphate) through a process called cellular respiration. This energy is used by the cell to carry out various functions such as growth, repair, and movement. The mitochondria also play a role in regulating the cell's metabolism and calcium levels, and are involved in the process of apoptosis, or programmed cell death.
