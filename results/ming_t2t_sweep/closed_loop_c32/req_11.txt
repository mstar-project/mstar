The main causes of World War I were militarism, alliances, imperialism, and nationalism. Militarism was the belief that a strong military was necessary for a nation's security and success. Alliances were agreements between countries to provide mutual protection in the event of war. Imperialism was the practice of expanding a nation's power and influence through colonization or other means. Nationalism was the belief that one's own nation was superior to others. These four factors combined to create an environment in which war was seen as a viable option for resolving disputes.
