"1984" is a dystopian novel by George Orwell, published in 1949. The story is set in a totalitarian society ruled by the Party, led by Big Brother, where individuality and independent thought are suppressed. The protagonist, Winston Smith, works for the Party rewriting history to fit its propaganda. He becomes disillusioned with the oppressive regime and begins a forbidden affair with Julia, a coworker. They seek out a rumored resistance movement, but are betrayed and captured by the Thought Police. Under torture, Winston betrays Julia and is brainwashed into loving Big Brother. The novel explores themes of surveillance, truth, and the power of language in controlling thought.
