Dehydration occurs when your body loses more fluids than it takes in, and it can range from mild to severe. The symptoms of dehydration can vary depending on the severity, but common signs include:

### Mild to Moderate Dehydration:
1. **Thirst**: One of the earliest and most obvious signs.
2. **Dry Mouth and Mucous Membranes**: Your mouth and throat may feel dry.
3. **Fatigue**: Feeling unusually tired or lethargic.
4. **Decreased Urine Output**: Producing less urine than usual, and it may be dark yellow.
5. **Dizziness or Lightheadedness**: Especially when standing up quickly.
6. **Headache**: Mild to moderate headaches can occur.
7. **Dry Skin**: Skin may lose its elasticity and feel dry.
8. **Muscle Cramps**: Particularly in the legs and arms.
9. **Sunken Eyes**: Eyes may appear sunken or hollow.
10. **Rapid Heartbeat**: Heart rate may increase as the body tries to compensate for lower blood volume.
