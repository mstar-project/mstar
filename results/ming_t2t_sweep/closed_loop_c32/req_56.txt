A CV (Curriculum Vitae) and a resume are both documents that provide an overview of a person's professional background, education, and skills. However, there are some key differences between the two:

1. Length: A CV is typically longer than a resume, often running several pages, while a resume is usually one or two pages long.

2. Purpose: A CV is used primarily for academic, scientific, or research positions, while a resume is used for most other types of jobs.

3. Content: A CV includes a detailed account of a person's entire career history, including all jobs, internships, and volunteer work, as well as their education, publications, presentations, and other relevant information. A resume, on the other hand, is a more concise summary of a person's most relevant experience and skills for a specific job.

4. Format: A CV is typically formatted in a chronological order, listing all of a person's experiences in reverse chronological order. A resume can be formatted in various ways, including chronological, functional, or combination formats.

5. Customization: A CV is usually not customized for each job application, while a resume is often tailored to fit the specific requirements of each job.
