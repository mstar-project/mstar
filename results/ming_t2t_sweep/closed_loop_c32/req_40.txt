1. Ad Hominem: This is a fallacy where the argument attacks the person making the argument rather than addressing the argument itself. For example, if someone says "You can't trust John's opinion on climate change because he drives a gas-guzzling car," they are committing an ad hominem fallacy.

2. False Dilemma: This is a fallacy where only two options are presented as the only possible choices, when in fact there may be other options available. For example, if someone says "Either you support this new law or you're against progress," they are presenting a false dilemma.

3. Slippery Slope: This is a fallacy where it is assumed that one event will inevitably lead to another, without any evidence to support this assumption. For example, if someone says "If we allow same-sex marriage, next thing you know people will want to marry their pets," they are committing a slippery slope fallacy.
