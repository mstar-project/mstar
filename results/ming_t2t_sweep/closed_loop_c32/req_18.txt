The water cycle is the continuous movement of water on, above and below the surface of the Earth. It is driven by solar energy and involves evaporation, condensation, precipitation, infiltration, runoff and transpiration. Evaporation occurs when liquid water is heated by the sun and turns into water vapor. Condensation occurs when water vapor cools and forms clouds. Precipitation occurs when the water droplets in the clouds become too heavy and fall to the ground as rain, snow, hail or sleet. Infiltration is when the water seeps into the ground and replenishes groundwater supplies. Runoff is when the water flows over the land and eventually makes its way back to the ocean. Transpiration is when plants absorb water from the soil and release it into the atmosphere through their leaves.
