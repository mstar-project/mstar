Latitude and longitude are two of the most important concepts in geography and navigation. They are used to specify the exact location of a point on the Earth's surface. Here's how they differ:

1. **Definition:**
   - **Latitude** refers to the angular distance of a point north or south of the equator. It is measured in degrees, with 0° at the equator and 90° at the poles (North Pole and South Pole).
   - **Longitude** refers to the angular distance of a point east or west of the Prime Meridian, which passes through Greenwich, England. It is also measured in degrees, with 0° at the Prime Meridian and 180° at the International Date Line.

2. **Direction:**
   - Latitude lines run horizontally around the Earth, parallel to the equator. They are also known as parallels.
   - Longitude lines run vertically from the North Pole to the South Pole. They are also known as meridians.
