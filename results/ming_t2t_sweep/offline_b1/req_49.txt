"1984" is a dystopian novel by George Orwell, published in 1949. The story is set in a totalitarian society ruled by the Party, led by Big Brother, where individuality and independent thought are suppressed. The protagonist, Winston Smith, works at the Ministry of Truth, where he alters historical records to fit the Party's propaganda.

Winston becomes disillusioned with the oppressive regime and begins a forbidden affair with Julia, a coworker. They seek out a secret resistance movement, but are betrayed and captured by the Thought Police. Under torture, Winston is forced to betray Julia and ultimately submits to the Party's ideology.

The novel explores themes of surveillance, propaganda, and the manipulation of truth, as well as the dangers of totalitarianism and the loss of individual freedom. It ends with Winston's complete submission to the Party, symbolized by his love for Big Brother.
