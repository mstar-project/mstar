A hypothesis is an educated guess or an idea that can be tested through experimentation or observation. It is a tentative explanation for a phenomenon or a set of observations that can be proven or disproven through further investigation. A hypothesis is often used as a starting point for scientific inquiry and is typically formulated in a way that allows it to be tested and potentially falsified.

On the other hand, a theory is a well-substantiated explanation of some aspect of the natural world, based on a body of facts that have been repeatedly confirmed through observation and experimentation. A theory is not just a guess or a hunch; it is a comprehensive framework that explains a wide range of phenomena and is supported by a large body of evidence. Theories are often developed over time as hypotheses are tested and refined, and they can be modified or even replaced if new evidence contradicts them.

In summary, a hypothesis is a proposed explanation that can be tested, while a theory is a well-supported explanation that has been extensively tested and is widely accepted by the scientific community.
