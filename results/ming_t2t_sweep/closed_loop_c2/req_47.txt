A combustion engine is an internal combustion engine that converts the chemical energy stored in fuel into mechanical energy. The process begins with the intake of air and fuel into the engine's combustion chamber. Once inside, the air-fuel mixture is compressed by the piston and then ignited by a spark plug. This causes a rapid expansion of gases, which pushes the piston down and turns the crankshaft. The crankshaft is connected to the drive shaft, which ultimately powers the vehicle. The exhaust gases are then expelled from the engine through the exhaust system.
