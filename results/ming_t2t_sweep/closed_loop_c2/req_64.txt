Rainbows are caused by the refraction, reflection, and dispersion of light in water droplets. When sunlight passes through a water droplet, it is bent and separated into its component colors, creating a spectrum of colors that we see as a rainbow. The angle at which the light is refracted and reflected determines the position and shape of the rainbow. Rainbows typically appear when the sun is behind the observer and there are raindrops in the air in front of them.
