The scientific method is a systematic approach to problem-solving that involves several steps. Here are the main steps of the scientific method:

1. **Observation**: The first step in the scientific method is to make observations about the world around you. This can involve noticing patterns, making measurements, or gathering data.

2. **Question**: Based on your observations, you may have questions or wonder why something happens the way it does. This is where you formulate a question that you want to answer through your investigation.

3. **Hypothesis**: A hypothesis is an educated guess or a proposed explanation for the question you have. It should be testable and falsifiable, meaning that it can be proven wrong through experimentation or observation.

4. **Experimentation**: To test your hypothesis, you need to design and conduct an experiment. This involves manipulating variables and measuring the results to see if they support or refute your hypothesis.

5. **Data Collection**: During the experiment, you will collect data, which can include measurements, observations, and other information that will help you analyze the results.
