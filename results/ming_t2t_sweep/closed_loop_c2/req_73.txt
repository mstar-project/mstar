Dark matter is a type of matter that does not interact with light or other forms of electromagnetic radiation, making it invisible to telescopes and other instruments that detect light. It is thought to make up about 85% of the matter in the universe, but its exact nature is still unknown. Scientists believe that dark matter exists because of its gravitational effects on visible matter, such as stars and galaxies. The presence of dark matter is inferred from its gravitational pull on visible matter, which causes galaxies to rotate faster than they would if only visible matter were present. Despite extensive research, scientists have not yet been able to directly detect or identify dark matter particles, making it one of the biggest mysteries in modern physics.
