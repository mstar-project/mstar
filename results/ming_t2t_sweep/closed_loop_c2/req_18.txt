The water cycle is the continuous movement of water on, above and below the surface of the Earth. It is driven by solar energy and involves evaporation, condensation, precipitation, infiltration, runoff, and subsurface flow. Evaporation occurs when liquid water is heated by the sun and turns into water vapor. Condensation occurs when water vapor cools and forms clouds. Precipitation occurs when the water droplets in the clouds become too heavy and fall to the ground as rain, snow, hail, or sleet. Infiltration is when the water seeps into the ground, while runoff is when the water flows over the surface of the Earth. Subsurface flow is when the water moves underground. The water cycle is essential for life on Earth, as it helps to regulate climate, transport nutrients, and provide fresh water for plants and animals.
