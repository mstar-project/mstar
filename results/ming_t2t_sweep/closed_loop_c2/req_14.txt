The sun had just dipped below the horizon, casting a warm orange glow over the rugged coastline. The waves crashed against the rocks, sending up plumes of white foam that danced in the fading light. In the distance, the lighthouse stood tall and proud, its beam cutting through the gathering darkness like a beacon of hope.

Inside the lighthouse, the keeper, a grizzled old man named Jack, sat at his desk, surrounded by stacks of dusty books and maps. He had been the keeper of this lighthouse for over thirty years, and he knew every inch of the rocky shore like the back of his hand. But tonight, something felt different. The air was thick with an eerie stillness, and the wind howled through the narrow windows, as if warning him of an impending storm.

As Jack made his nightly rounds, checking the oil lamps and ensuring that the beacon was functioning properly, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was watching him from the shadows. He quickened his pace, his heart pounding in his chest, as he made his way to the top of the lighthouse, where the lens was located.

As he reached the top, he saw it - a figure standing on the edge of the cliff, silhouetted against the moonlight
