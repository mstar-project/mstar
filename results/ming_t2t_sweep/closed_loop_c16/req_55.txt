Wi-Fi is a wireless networking technology that allows devices to connect to the internet without the need for physical cables. It works by using radio waves to transmit data between a wireless router and devices such as computers, smartphones, and tablets.

Here's a simplified explanation of how Wi-Fi works:

1. **Wireless Router**: The central component of a Wi-Fi network is the wireless router. This device connects to your internet service provider (ISP) via a wired connection (such as a cable or DSL line) and then broadcasts a wireless signal.

2. **Radio Waves**: The router sends out radio waves in the form of electromagnetic signals. These signals are transmitted on specific frequencies, typically 2.4 GHz or 5 GHz, which are part of the radio spectrum.

3. **Devices**: Devices with Wi-Fi capabilities, such as laptops, smartphones, and smart TVs, have wireless adapters that can detect and connect to these radio waves. When a device is within range of the router, it can pick up the signal and establish a connection.
