The key principles of object-oriented programming (OOP) are encapsulation, abstraction, inheritance, and polymorphism. Encapsulation is the practice of bundling data and methods that operate on that data within a single unit, such as a class. Abstraction is the process of hiding the implementation details of an object from the user. Inheritance allows a class to inherit properties and methods from another class. Polymorphism is the ability of objects to take on multiple forms, allowing for code reuse and flexibility.
