Moore's Law is the observation that the number of transistors in a dense integrated circuit doubles approximately every two years. This trend has continued for more than 50 years, and it is expected to continue for the foreseeable future. The law is named after Gordon E. Moore, one of the co-founders of Intel Corporation, who first observed this trend in 1965. Moore's Law has been a driving force behind the rapid advancement of technology and the exponential growth of computing power over the past several decades. However, as the size of transistors approaches atomic scales, there are physical limitations that may eventually slow down or halt the progress predicted by Moore's Law.
