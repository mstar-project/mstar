The main causes of World War I were militarism, alliances, imperialism, and nationalism. Militarism was a major factor as countries built up their armies and navies in preparation for war. Alliances between countries also played a role, as countries formed alliances with each other in order to protect themselves from potential enemies. Imperialism was another factor, as countries sought to expand their empires by taking control of other countries. Finally, nationalism was a major cause of World War I, as countries sought to assert their dominance over other nations.
