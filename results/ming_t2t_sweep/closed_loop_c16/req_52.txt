A sonnet is a poem that consists of 14 lines, typically written in iambic pentameter. It is usually divided into four quatrains and a final couplet, with a rhyme scheme of ABAB CDCD EFEF GG. The sonnet form originated in Italy in the 13th century and was popularized by poets such as Petrarch and Shakespeare. Sonnets often explore themes of love, beauty, and mortality, and are known for their use of metaphor, imagery, and other literary devices.
