The trolley problem is a thought experiment in ethics that involves a runaway trolley heading towards five people tied to a track. You are standing next to a lever that can divert the trolley onto another track, where there is one person tied up. The question is whether you should pull the lever, sacrificing one person to save five, or do nothing and let the trolley kill the five people. The problem raises questions about the morality of sacrificing one person to save many, and whether it is better to actively cause harm or passively allow harm to occur. It has been used as a tool for exploring ethical theories such as utilitarianism and deontology.
