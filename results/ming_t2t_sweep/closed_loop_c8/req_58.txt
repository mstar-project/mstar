CRISPR (Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats) is a revolutionary gene-editing technology that has transformed the field of genetics and biotechnology. It's a powerful tool that allows scientists to edit genes with unprecedented precision, efficiency, and flexibility. Here's a simplified explanation of what CRISPR is and how it works:

**What is CRISPR?**

CRISPR is a natural defense mechanism found in bacteria and other microorganisms. It's a system that protects these organisms from viral infections by recognizing and destroying the viral DNA. The CRISPR system consists of two main components:

1. **Cas9**: an enzyme that acts like molecular scissors, cutting DNA at specific locations.
2. **Guide RNA (gRNA)**: a small piece of RNA that helps the Cas9 enzyme find the target DNA sequence.
