The moon affects ocean tides by its gravitational pull. The gravitational force of the moon pulls on the water in the Earth's oceans, causing them to bulge out towards the moon. This creates a high tide on the side of the Earth facing the moon and another high tide on the opposite side of the Earth. As the Earth rotates, different areas experience high and low tides. The sun also has an effect on tides, but its gravitational force is weaker than that of the moon, so it has less of an impact on tides.
