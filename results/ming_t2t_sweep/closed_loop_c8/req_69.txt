Noise-canceling headphones use a combination of passive and active noise cancellation to reduce unwanted ambient sounds. Passive noise cancellation is achieved through the physical design of the headphones, which blocks out sound waves from entering the ear. Active noise cancellation, on the other hand, uses microphones to pick up external noise and then generates an opposite sound wave to cancel it out. This process is known as destructive interference, where two sound waves of equal amplitude but opposite phase cancel each other out, resulting in a significant reduction of noise. The result is a quieter listening experience, allowing you to focus on your music or calls without being disturbed by background noise.
