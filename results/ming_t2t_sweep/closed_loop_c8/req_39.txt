A hypothesis is an educated guess or a proposed explanation for a phenomenon, while a theory is a well-substantiated explanation of some aspect of the natural world that is based on a body of evidence. A hypothesis is a starting point for further investigation, while a theory is a comprehensive explanation that has been extensively tested and supported by a large body of evidence. In other words, a hypothesis is a tentative explanation that can be tested, while a theory is a well-established explanation that has been repeatedly confirmed through observation and experimentation.
