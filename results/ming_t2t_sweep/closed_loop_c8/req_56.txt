A CV (Curriculum Vitae) and a resume are both documents used to present a person's qualifications, experience, and skills to potential employers. However, there are some key differences between the two:

1. Length: A CV is typically longer than a resume, often running several pages, while a resume is usually one or two pages long.

2. Purpose: A CV is used primarily for academic, scientific, or research positions, while a resume is used for most other types of jobs.

3. Content: A CV includes a detailed account of a person's education, work history, publications, presentations, awards, and other relevant information. A resume, on the other hand, focuses on a person's most relevant work experience and skills for a specific job.

4. Format: A CV is usually formatted in a chronological order, listing all of a person's education and work history in reverse chronological order. A resume can be formatted in various ways, including chronological, functional, or combination formats.

5. Customization: A CV is typically not customized for each job application, while a resume is often tailored to fit the specific requirements of each job.
